KPI dictionary
Every metric MedBridge tracks, defined by formula, source, audience, and owner. The North Star is Understanding Rate — all other KPIs support or explain it.
Audience 1 — Patient understanding
Understanding Rate — North Star Metric
PatientEllijahMVP must-have
The percentage of summary feedback responses where the patient selected "Yes, this helped me understand." This is the single most important metric. Every other KPI is either upstream (did the product work well enough to generate feedback?) or downstream (what happened after the patient understood?).
Yes responses ÷ total feedback submitted × 100
Source: summary_feedback.understanding_rating  |  Owner: Backend (view) + Frontend (widget)
Confidence Score
PatientEllijahMVP must-have
Average self-reported confidence (1–5 stars) on "How prepared do you feel to discuss this with your provider?" Measures activation, not just comprehension. A patient can understand a summary but still feel unequipped to talk to their doctor — this captures that gap.
SUM(confidence_rating) ÷ COUNT(feedback submitted)
Source: summary_feedback.confidence_rating (column to add in migration 006)  |  Owner: Backend + Frontend
Summary Helpfulness Score
PatientMVP must-have
Combined Yes + Somewhat rate. The broader positive signal — captures patients who got partial help, not just those who fully understood. Useful for identifying document types that are partially comprehensible vs fully opaque.
(Yes + Somewhat) ÷ total feedback × 100
Source: summary_feedback  |  Owner: Backend
Questions Asked Per Document
PatientEllijahMVP must-have
Average number of Q&A messages per uploaded document. A proxy for active engagement — patients who ask questions are working toward understanding, not passively scrolling. High value = deep engagement. Zero = patient may have been confused but didn't know they could ask.
Total chat messages ÷ total documents with any chat activity
Source: chat_messages, health_records  |  Owner: Backend
AI Chat Satisfaction Score
PatientMVP must-have
Average patient rating (1–5) on individual chat answers. Already tracked in kpi_ai_satisfaction view (live). Measures quality of AI responses from the patient's perspective, not technical correctness. Low score = answers may be too technical, too long, or unhelpful.
AVG(feedback_rating) WHERE feedback_rating IS NOT NULL
Source: chat_messages.feedback_rating  |  KPI view: kpi_ai_satisfaction (live)
Provider Readiness Score
ReadinessEllijahSprint 2
Rate of patients who used Appointment Prep after receiving a summary. This is the final and most meaningful step in the MedBridge journey — the moment confusion becomes conversation readiness. Also the metric most directly tied to CMS patient activation requirements.
Users who generated prep questions ÷ users who received a summary × 100
Source: follow_ups, summaries  |  Owner: Backend
Audience 2 — Ellijah / CMS stakeholder
Upload Success Rate
EllijahTeamMVP must-have
Percentage of document uploads that complete extraction and reach summarized status. From Ellijah's perspective: if patients can't get their documents processed, MedBridge isn't helping anyone. Low rate = barrier before the product even starts working.
Documents with status = 'summarized' ÷ total uploads × 100
Source: health_records.status  |  Owner: Backend
Patient Journey Completion Rate
EllijahMVP must-have
Percentage of patients who complete the full journey: upload → summary → feedback submitted. This is the CMS-relevant engagement metric — documented, patient-reported activation. Not just logins, not just uploads. Full completion.
Users who submitted feedback ÷ users who uploaded at least one document × 100
Source: summary_feedback, health_records  |  Owner: Backend
Return User Rate
EllijahSprint 2
Percentage of registered users who upload documents across multiple distinct sessions. Demonstrates sustained engagement rather than one-time use — the difference between a useful tool and a tool patients trust enough to come back to.
Users with uploads on 2+ distinct days ÷ total registered users × 100
Source: health_records, app_events  |  Owner: Backend
Feature Adoption Rate
EllijahSprint 2
Percentage of active users with at least one optional feature enabled: Reminders, Trusted Contacts, or MyChart Integration. CMS treats optional feature adoption as evidence of meaningful member engagement — not passive enrollment.
Users with any feature enabled ÷ total active users × 100
Source: user_settings  |  Owner: Backend + Frontend (toggles)
Audience 3 — Team / operational
OCR / Extraction Failure Rate
TeamMVP must-have
Percentage of uploads that fail at text extraction. Track broken down by failure reason (unsupported file type, image quality too low, file too large) and by file type (PDF vs phone photo). Phone photo failure rate is the most important — that's where low-income patients are most affected.
Documents with status = 'failed' ÷ total uploads × 100
Source: health_records.status, health_records.failure_reason  |  Owner: Backend
Reading Level Pass Rate
TeamMVP must-have
Percentage of summaries scoring at or below 6th grade Flesch-Kincaid level. Already live in kpi_reading_level view. Target: greater than 90% at grade 6 or below. If below target, the summary prompt needs adjustment — not the frontend.
Summaries with reading_level_score ≤ 6 ÷ total summaries × 100
Source: summaries.reading_level_score  |  KPI view: kpi_reading_level (live)
Guardrail Pass Rate
TeamMVP must-have
Percentage of AI responses that pass all safety checks: no diagnosis language, no treatment recommendations, provider disclaimer present. Already live in kpi_quality view. Must be 100% on any response that contains medical terminology. Non-negotiable.
Summaries with quality_passed = true ÷ total summaries × 100
Source: summaries.quality_passed  |  KPI view: kpi_quality (live)
Missing Feedback Rate
TeamMVP must-have
Percentage of summaries with no associated feedback record. High rate means patients aren't seeing or using the feedback widget. This is simultaneously a data quality problem (North Star KPI has no numerator) and a UX problem (widget is not prominent enough).
Summaries with no summary_feedback row ÷ total summaries × 100
Source: summaries LEFT JOIN summary_feedback  |  Owner: Backend + Frontend

Patient activation framework
MedBridge doesn't just measure understanding — it measures whether understanding leads to action. This framework adapts Patient Activation Measure (PAM) principles for a document-understanding app.
The goal isn't a patient who read a summary. The goal is a patient who walks into their provider appointment knowing what to ask.
Activation levels
Level 1

Passive — uploaded and read

Patient uploads document, receives summary, takes no further action. Measures: summary viewed, no feedback submitted, no chat, no prep. This patient may have been helped but we have no evidence of it.

Level 2

Aware — acknowledged understanding

Patient submits understanding feedback. Even a "Somewhat" response signals awareness. Measures: summary_feedback.understanding_rating is not null. This is the minimum for the North Star KPI to have data.

Level 3

Engaged — asked follow-up questions

Patient sent at least one Q&A message about their document. Measures: chat_messages count greater than 0 for this document. This patient is actively working toward understanding, not just passively receiving it.

Level 4

Activated — ready to talk to their provider

Patient generated appointment prep questions AND rated confidence 4 or 5. This is the full MedBridge outcome: from confused patient to prepared patient. Measures: follow_ups row exists + confidence_rating ≥ 4.

Reporting layers
Three reporting layers with different audiences, cadences, and tones. Patient report = encouraging. Client report = outcomes and ROI. Team report = failures and fixes.
Patient — monthly progress report

Your Understanding Progress — monthly summary

A plain-language summary shown in the patient dashboard or sent via reminder notification. Modeled on myAir's nightly summary. Tone: warm, encouraging, specific. No clinical language. No error rates.

  • Documents reviewed this month
  • Understanding score trend — improving / stable / needs attention
  • Questions asked this month
  • Confidence trend — how prepared you're feeling over time
  • Gentle nudge toward appointment prep if not yet used
  • Reminder that saved questions are ready for their next appointment

Reading level target: 4th–5th grade. Tone: personal and encouraging. Never say "your data shows" — say "you've been doing great this month."

The one-page Ellijah pitch

The problem MedBridge solves — in Ellijah's terms

Patients leave appointments confused. They receive discharge paperwork, lab results, and medication instructions they cannot understand. They don't know what to do next. They don't know what to ask their provider. And healthcare organizations have no documented evidence that patients understood anything they were given.

CMS now requires that healthcare organizations serving Medicare and Medicaid patients actively support health literacy and member engagement — and produce evidence that they're doing it.

MedBridge delivers that evidence. Every time a patient says "Yes, this helped me understand my document," that is a patient-reported health literacy outcome a healthcare organization can point to. It is the exact type of engagement data that CAHPS measures and that CMS uses to score health plan quality.

That is the business case. MedBridge is not just a helpful app. It is a documented patient engagement and health literacy program that a healthcare organization serving Medicaid and Medicare patients can use to demonstrate CMS compliance.

Variation patterns that make the data realistic

User behavior archetypes to simulate

  • The confused patient: uploads document, views summary, rates "No" for understanding, sends 5+ chat questions, never uses prep. ~15% of patients.
  • The activated patient: uploads, views summary, rates "Yes", generates prep questions, confidence rating 4–5. ~20% of patients.
  • The passive patient: uploads document, receives summary, takes no further action — no feedback, no chat. ~30% of patients.
  • The repeat user: uploads multiple documents across multiple sessions, varying understanding scores. ~20% of patients.
  • The feature adopter: enables reminders and trusted contacts, returns weekly. ~15% of patients.
